Call To Action!

Volunteering

Joining the fight against climate change through volunteering is a powerful way for individuals to make a real difference. When you volunteer, you actively contribute to efforts like preserving ecosystems, reducing carbon footprints, and supporting sustainable practices. Whether you're planting trees, participating in clean-up initiatives, or advocating for eco-friendly policies, your actions add up to create positive impacts locally and globally. Beyond the environmental benefits, volunteering builds a sense of community and shared responsibility, inspiring others to get involved. Each volunteer has the potential to create meaningful change, driving significant progress in the battle against climate change. You are able to contribute to innovative solutions, making your involvement even more important in shaping a more sustainable and resilient planet. Join us in taking meaningful steps towards a greener and healthier planet!

Title: Citizen's Climate Lobby
Location: Chicagoland Area
Web: citizensclimatelobby.org
Citizens' Climate Lobby (CCL) is a non-profit organization dedicated to advocating for effective climate policies. With a grassroots approach, CCL empowers citizens to engage with their elected representatives, fostering bipartisan support for legislative solutions to address climate change. Through volunteer efforts and a commitment to building relationships with policymakers, CCL aims to promote the adoption of fair and sustainable policies to reduce carbon emissions and safeguard the environment.
Title: Chicagoland Environmental Network
Location: Chicagoland Area
Web: chicagoenvironment.org
Where do you go when you care about the natural world and want to get more involved? Maybe you already recycle but want to do more. Or perhaps you're a teacher and you want your students to understand—and help protect—the natural resources around them. Possibly you work for an environmental organization and want to reach other organizations with the same goals. The Chicagoland Environmental Network (CEN), a group of more than 240 environmental and natural resource organizations, can get you connected.
Title: Sierra Club Chicago Group
Location: Chicagoland Area
Web: sierraclub.org/illinois/chicago
The Sierra Club was founded in 1892 and is dedicated to the protection and preservation of the environment. It engages in various activities and campaigns aimed at promoting clean energy, addressing climate change, protecting public lands, advocating for environmental justice, and supporting biodiversity. The organization encourages sustainable practices and policies while promoting outdoor activities and appreciation for nature.
Title: Aliance for the Great Lakes
Location: All Five Great Lakes
Web: greatlakes.org
The Alliance for the Great Lakes works to address environmental issues affecting the Great Lakes region, including water quality, habitat restoration, pollution prevention, and sustainable water management. The organization collaborates with various stakeholders, including communities, policymakers, and businesses, to advocate for policies and practices that ensure the long-term health of the Great Lakes ecosystem.
Title: Greenpeace Chicago
Location: Chicago (Online)
Web: greenpeace.org
Greenpeace was founded in 1971 and operates as a non-governmental organization (NGO) with a focus on environmental protection, conservation, and activism. The organization is known for its direct action campaigns and peaceful protests to address issues such as climate change, deforestation, overfishing, pollution, and nuclear power. Their website contains a wide list of different petitions to sign which takes almost no time from your day.
Title: Plant Chicago
Location: Chicagoland Area
Web: plantchicago.org
Plant Chicago is a non-profit organization contributing to addressing climate change through its focus on sustainable and circular economy practices. Emphasizing resource efficiency, the organization uses closed-loop systems where waste becomes a valuable resource, reducing overall environmental impact. Additionally, Plant Chicago may integrate renewable energy practices and promote their adoption within businesses and communities
